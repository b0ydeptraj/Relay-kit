# Storytelling Design

Use narrative design only when the product benefits from pacing, reveal, or atmosphere. Most product UI does not need a spectacle layer.

## When storytelling helps
- landing pages that need emotional framing
- brand or portfolio surfaces with a clear narrative arc
- launch pages where reveal order matters
- editorial or campaign work where atmosphere is part of the message

## When to stay restrained
- dashboards
- forms
- settings
- checkout flows
- dense operator surfaces

## Useful narrative moves
- reveal sections in a deliberate order
- use depth or contrast changes to show progression
- let imagery or illustration carry mood while text stays tight
- keep CTA timing obvious; do not hide action behind theatrics

## Guardrails
- story should support the task, not block it
- peak moments should be rare and intentional
- performance and accessibility still win over flair
- remove narrative effects if they weaken scanning speed or clarity
